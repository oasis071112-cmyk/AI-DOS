# Glossary

This document defines the terminology used throughout AI-DOS.

---

## Agent

An AI system that can reason, plan, and complete tasks.

Examples include ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Cursor, and other AI coding assistants.

---

## Skill

A reusable capability that teaches an AI agent how to perform a specific task.

A Skill loads the required knowledge and applies it to solve a problem.

---

## Knowledge

Structured design knowledge.

Knowledge includes design principles, UX, visual language, accessibility, engineering practices, and quality standards.

Knowledge is the foundation of AI-DOS.

---

## Pattern

A reusable solution for a common product or page structure.

Examples include landing pages, dashboards, pricing pages, authentication flows, and documentation sites.

---

## Component

A reusable UI building block.

Examples include buttons, cards, navigation bars, forms, tables, and dialogs.

---

## Token

A reusable design value.

Examples include colors, typography, spacing, radius, shadows, and motion values.

---

## Review

A quality assurance process that evaluates generated designs.

Review ensures consistency, usability, accessibility, and performance.

---

## Design Language

The visual and interaction rules shared across an entire product.

---

## Design System

A collection of reusable components, guidelines, and tokens used to build consistent interfaces.

---

## Design Operating System

A structured knowledge framework that teaches AI how to think, design, build, and review digital products.
